01 — About

Who I Am

I'm a Mechatronics and Control Engineer with a deep focus on embedded systems and electronics. My work lives at the intersection of hardware design, low-level firmware, and industrial control — writing code that runs directly on silicon and building circuits that drive real machinery.

"Reliable hardware isn't magic — it's the result of understanding every layer from transistor to protocol."

From writing bare-metal C for ARM Cortex-M devices to designing PCB layouts, configuring communication buses like SPI, I2C, UART, and CAN, and building PLC automation for factory floors — I bring precision and depth to every layer of the stack.

I'm currently active at both PITB (Punjab Information Technology Board) on embedded firmware and NEWAY Industries on industrial automation, giving me an unusually broad cross-domain view of real-world hardware engineering.
